# Teamfight Positioning Guide

## Core Principles

As the team's main damage dealer, your positioning in teamfights directly determines match outcomes. Three core principles: **Survival > Damage output, Position = Information, Engagement timing > Equipment**.

## Positioning Fundamentals

### 1. Maintain Safe Distance

Always stay behind your frontline (tanks/bruisers) by a comfortable margin. This distance ensures:
- 1-2 seconds reaction time if the frontline is breached
- Enemy assassins must commit mobility skills to reach you
- You can consistently damage the enemy frontline

### 2. Flanking vs Backline Positioning

**Backline positioning** for: when enemy dive is unaccounted for, when your team has sufficient crowd control.
**Flanking** for: when you need to bypass enemy frontline, when coordinating with a teammate's flank.

Beginners should stay in the backline 90% of the time; flanking carries extreme risk.

### 3. Using Terrain

- Prioritize positions with wall protection on one side to reduce flank angles
- Bushes are double-edged: they hide your position but may be camped
- Objective fights: stay within tower/safe zone range to force enemies to overcommit

## Teamfight Phase Positioning

### Pre-fight (0-3s)
- Confirm all 5 enemy positions before committing to a position
- If enemy assassin/fighter is missing, do not walk forward
- Pre-plan your escape route (dash direction, retreat path)

### Mid-fight (3-10s)
- Attack the closest target; do not chase past tanks to reach backline
- Save mobility skills for escape, never engage with them
- Recheck minimap every 2 seconds: where is the enemy assassin?
- Keep moving — standing still to DPS equals death

### Cleanup (10s+)
- Your frontline dies → retreat immediately
- Enemy rout → chase in formation, never solo chase deep
- Low HP → recall decisively; greed for one wave can cost the next objective

## Common Mistakes

1. **Standing too far forward**: trading one auto-attack for frontline position
2. **Ignoring the minimap**: getting flanked with zero preparation
3. **Wasting mobility skills**: using dash/flash to chase instead of survive
4. **Turret-mode DPS**: not adjusting position, getting one-shot when enemy CDs come up

## Pre-fight Checklist

Before every teamfight, mentally confirm:
- [ ] Where is the enemy assassin?
- [ ] What are my escape cooldowns?
- [ ] What is my retreat path?
- [ ] Who is my primary target this fight?
